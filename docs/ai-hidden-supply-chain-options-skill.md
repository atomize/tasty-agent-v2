## Step 3 — The 7 Supply Chain Layers

Analyze and provide trade ideas for each of these 7 layers every time.
For each layer, explain WHY it's a choke point, then give specific trade ideas.

### LAYER 1 — CHIP PACKAGING & INSPECTION
**Why it's a choke point:** Advanced 2.5D packaging (required for all modern AI chips) has
almost no capacity outside TSMC (sold out) and Amkor. HBM memory stacks require inspection
at every single layer — Camtek is the dominant tool vendor.

**Core watchlist:**
- AMKR (Amkor Technology) — 2.5D advanced packaging, Arizona + Vietnam capacity expansion
- CAMT (Camtek) — HBM wafer inspection, HBM4 transition catalyst
- ACMR (ACM Research) — wafer cleaning equipment for AI fabs

**What to look for:** New packaging customer wins at Amkor; HBM4 production ramp dates;
any TSMC CoWoS capacity news (constrains or expands AMKR opportunity).

---

### LAYER 2 — OPTICAL INTERCONNECTS & TRANSCEIVERS
**Why it's a choke point:** At 100,000+ GPU cluster scale, copper wiring physically
cannot carry the signal. Every rack, every data center needs optical transceivers.
The industry is mid-cycle upgrading from 800G to 1.6T — a product so complex that
only Fabrinet can manufacture it reliably at scale.

**Core watchlist:**
- FN (Fabrinet) — dominant contract manufacturer for optical transceivers, 1.6T ramp
- CIEN (Ciena) — optical networking for inter-data center connectivity
- LITE (Lumentum) — optical switches and laser components for AI servers

**What to look for:** 1.6T volume ramp timeline at Fabrinet; Fabrinet "Building 10" Thailand
capacity online date; any supply shortage news for optical components.

---

### LAYER 3 — SIGNAL INTEGRITY & AI CONNECTIVITY
**Why it's a choke point:** As data speeds increase, electrical signals degrade over
shorter and shorter distances. Retimer chips "clean and boost" the signal so data can
reach the GPU. PCIe Gen 6 deployment makes this market structurally larger every year.

**Core watchlist:**
- ALAB (Astera Labs) — PCIe retimers, Scorpio AI fabric switches, NVLink Fusion
- CRDO (Credo Technology) — competing retimer/connectivity chips, smaller and more volatile
- MRVL (Marvell Technology) — custom ASICs for AWS/Microsoft, 3nm volume production

**What to look for:** Astera Scorpio X high-volume production date (H2 2026);
PCIe Gen 6 server deployment timelines; Marvell ASIC revenue crossing from design wins to cash flow.

---

### LAYER 4 — AI RACK DEPLOYMENT & INTEGRATION
**Why it's a choke point:** Hyperscalers can design a data center on paper, but
someone has to physically integrate the racks, wiring, and systems. Celestica and EMCOR
are the contractors that turn GPU shipments into live compute capacity.

**Core watchlist:**
- CLS (Celestica) — rack integration, networking switches, ASIC manufacturing partner
- EME (EMCOR Group) — physical data center construction and electrical infrastructure
- CSCO (Cisco) — not hidden, but benefits from ultra-ethernet consortium specs in 2026

**What to look for:** Celestica quarterly guidance for CCS (Connectivity & Cloud Solutions)
segment; any new hyperscaler supply agreements; EMCOR data center contract wins.

---

### LAYER 5 — POWER & THERMAL MANAGEMENT
**Why it's a choke point:** Modern AI racks run at 50–100kW+. The entire installed
base of global data centers was built for 10–20kW racks. Every facility must be
retrofitted with liquid cooling and upgraded power distribution — there is no workaround.

**Core watchlist:**
- VRT (Vertiv) — UPS systems, liquid cooling, power distribution — the dominant player
- MOHN (Modine Manufacturing) — thermal management systems, smaller/more volatile than VRT
- NVT (nVent Electric) — electrical enclosures and thermal management for high-density racks

**What to look for:** Vertiv quarterly order book (forward visibility into data center spending);
liquid cooling adoption rate at hyperscalers; any new hyperscaler cooling contracts.

---

### LAYER 6 — RAW MATERIALS (COPPER & CRITICAL MINERALS)
**Why it's a choke point:** Every data center needs enormous quantities of copper for
wiring, transformers, and cooling. Data center demand for copper is described as "inelastic"
— developers pay whatever it costs. New copper mines take 15+ years to permit and build,
creating a structural multi-year deficit.

**Core watchlist:**
- FCX (Freeport-McMoRan) — largest publicly traded copper producer, most liquid options market
- COPX (Global X Copper Miners ETF) — diversified exposure if individual miner risk is too high
- MP (MP Materials) — rare earth materials, magnets for AI hardware and data center cooling fans

**What to look for:** Monthly International Copper Study Group supply-demand reports;
JP Morgan copper price target updates ($12,500/mt for Q2 2026); any major mine disruption news.

---

### LAYER 7 — NUCLEAR POWER & URANIUM
**Why it's a choke point:** AI data centers need always-on, baseload power.
Solar and wind are intermittent. Nuclear is the only carbon-free source that can deliver
24/7 power at the scale hyperscalers need. Uranium supply is structurally constrained —
new mines take years to permit, and utilities are locked into long-term contracts.

**Core watchlist:**
- CEG (Constellation Energy) — largest U.S. nuclear operator, hyperscaler PPAs with Microsoft/Amazon
- CCJ (Cameco) — world's largest listed uranium producer, 55% earnings growth projected 2026
- UEC (Uranium Energy Corp) — U.S.-based, leveraged to spot uranium, cheapest valuation in sector
- TLN (Talen Energy) — nuclear PPA deals with AI hyperscalers

**What to look for:** New hyperscaler nuclear PPA announcements; uranium spot price vs.
long-term contract price divergence; U.S. policy on nuclear permitting acceleration.

## Key Principles for Quality

- **These are not AI companies — they are AI enablers.** Always frame the analysis this way.
- **Specificity wins.** "Buy FN August $250 calls" beats "consider optical transceiver names."
- **Moat language matters.** Explain WHY each company is hard to replace — this is the core thesis.
- **Never force a trade.** If a layer has nothing compelling right now (e.g., copper is
  already priced for perfection), say so and flag it as a watch rather than a buy.
