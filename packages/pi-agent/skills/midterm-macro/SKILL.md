# Midterm Macro Options Playbook

Broader options plays across 5 sectors with a 30-90 day horizon. These positions are designed to capture macro catalysts while hedging against portfolio concentration in AI supply chain names.

## Energy Sector

Thesis: Geopolitical instability (Strait of Hormuz, OPEC+ discipline, Russia/Ukraine) creates persistent upside risk in oil. Energy is also a natural hedge against AI infrastructure costs (power prices).

| Ticker | Role | Catalyst | Options Play |
|--------|------|----------|-------------|
| XLE | Energy sector ETF | Broad sector exposure, diversified oil/gas | Buy calls on geopolitical escalation, sell puts on dips |
| XOM | Integrated oil major | Strait of Hormuz beneficiary, Guyana production ramp | Sell cash-secured puts at support, buy calls on breakouts |
| CVX | Integrated oil major | Permian Basin production, LNG export growth | Similar to XOM — pair trade on relative value |

**Hedging role**: Energy positions offset AI portfolio risk. If AI capex slows (bearish for Layers 1-7), energy demand is unaffected. If geopolitics spike oil, energy calls profit while tech may sell off.

## Defense and Aerospace

Thesis: European rearmament is a multi-decade spending cycle. U.S. defense budgets are bipartisan floor, not ceiling. Golden Dome missile defense and Patriot resupply are active catalysts.

| Ticker | Role | Catalyst | Options Play |
|--------|------|----------|-------------|
| RTX | Patriot missiles + Pratt engines | $50B Patriot contract pipeline, Strait of Hormuz intercepts | Buy calls ahead of contract announcements, sell puts at technical support |
| LMT | F-35 program + hypersonics | European F-35 orders, LRSO missile | Longer-dated calls (60-90 DTE) to capture contract cycles |
| NOC | B-21 Raider + space | B-21 production ramp, Golden Dome awards | Buy calls on budget announcements, calendar spreads around earnings |
| GD | Gulfstream + combat vehicles | Business jet demand + European rearmament | Sell puts — floor from backlog visibility |

**Hedging role**: Defense is counter-cyclical to tech. In a recession scenario, defense budgets hold while tech capex contracts. Long defense + long AI supply chain = natural diversification.

## Macro Bear / Hedges

These are portfolio protection plays. Not directional bets — insurance against drawdown.

| Ticker | Role | Catalyst | Options Play |

| QQQ | Tech-heavy index | Portfolio protection against tech selloff | Buy puts 5-10% OTM, 30-45 DTE, roll monthly. Cost: 0.5-1% of portfolio value |
| SPY | Broad market | Portfolio protection against systemic risk | Buy puts alongside QQQ puts for broader coverage |
| XRT | Consumer retail ETF | Consumer weakness indicator, short squeeze candidate | Buy puts as recession canary — if XRT breaks down, reduce long exposure |

## Cross-Sector Hedging Logic

The core principle: positions should offset each other across scenarios.

| Scenario | Winners | Losers | Net Effect |
|----------|---------|--------|------------|
| AI capex boom continues | Supply chain (L1-L7), AI semis | Hedges (QQQ puts expire worthless) | Strong positive, hedges are the cost of insurance |
| Geopolitical escalation | Energy (XOM, XLE), Defense (RTX, LMT) | Broad market (SPY dips) | Sector winners offset index pain |
| Recession / rate spike | Defense (floor), Hedges (QQQ/SPY puts pay) | AI supply chain, AI semis | Hedges protect portfolio, defense holds |
| AI capex pause | Hedges pay, Energy holds | Supply chain, AI semis | Managed drawdown via hedges |

When making recommendations:
1. Check current portfolio allocation across these scenarios
2. If overweight one scenario (e.g., all AI longs), recommend hedges first
3. Size new positions to maintain balanced exposure
4. Never let any single sector exceed 50% of buying power
